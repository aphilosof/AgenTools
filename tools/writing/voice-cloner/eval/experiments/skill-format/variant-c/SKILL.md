## Sentence Architecture
- Alternate between short punchy fragments ('There isn't.', 'Not one.') and longer compound-complex sentences — vary sharply, never monotonous.
- Open ~18% of sentences with conjunctions ('But', 'And', 'So') as normal starters; use conditional openers ('If you...', 'When you...') for advice.

## Voice & Register
- Conversational-intellectual register: plain Anglo-Saxon words ('get' not 'obtain', 'start' not 'commence'), contractions always ('don't', 'can't', 'it's', 'you're').
- Shift fluidly between 'I' (experience), 'you' (advice), and 'we' (shared); hedge with 'I suspect', 'perhaps', 'probably' — never 'clearly', 'obviously', or academic jargon.

## Argument Structure
- Bold claim first, then concrete examples immediately — never bury the lede or leave a principle unsupported.
- Pattern recognition across domains ('You see this pattern with X too'), preemptive counterarguments, rhetorical questions always answered.

## Paragraph Patterns
- Single-sentence paragraphs frequently for emphasis; one idea per paragraph, medium length.
- NEVER use: 'furthermore', 'moreover', 'in conclusion', 'it is worth noting that'.

## Rhetorical Fingerprints
- Rhetorical questions always answered; analogies from everyday life; parallel structure for emphasis; repeat key terms (never synonym-swap).
- Em dashes for interjections and mid-sentence examples; 'Ditto for' and 'The same is true for' as transitions. Never flowery metaphors.

## Formatting & Mechanics
- 0.8 commas per sentence — count them. Oxford comma always. Em dashes for parentheticals. Semicolons for related clauses.
- Never use: exclamation marks, bullet points in prose, headers/section breaks, ellipsis, bold text.

## Openings & Closings
- Open with a broad observation, counterintuitive claim, or personal discovery — never a definition or platitude.
- Close by reframing the core idea with accumulated insight — never 'In conclusion' or a call to action.

## Signature Phrases
- USE: in my experience, I suspect, it seems to me, I think so, as far as I can tell, in practice, in fact, which means, The same is true for, You see this pattern with, Ditto for, One difficulty here though, or more precisely, But this pattern isn't universal, And yet, Strangely enough, probably, my guess is
- NEVER USE: it is worth noting that, needless to say, furthermore, moreover, henceforth, utilize, leverage (as verb), paradigm, synergy, deep dive, at the end of the day, let's unpack, the bottom line is

## When Writing Essay
- Structure as continuous prose with no headers — ideas flow associatively.
- Endnotes in brackets [1], [2] for tangential thoughts — not citations.
